How Much of a Difference was Jaz Shelley’s Impact at Oregon Compared to Nebraska?

Huskers
Oregon
Shelley
Author

Kendall Moriarty

Published

February 11, 2025

Jaz Shelly had transferred to the University of Nebraska-Lincoln after two years at the University of Oregon. She has made a significant impact to the team since the moment she stepped onto campus. Shelley decided after last season that she was going to stay to take her fifth year. Being around her every day and being able to play with her since she got here, I can see that she is a star on both sides of the court.

The question is, how much of a difference was her impact at Oregon compared to Nebraska?

Let’s take a look at last season’s data and see how she is impacting the Nebraska Cornhuskers.

library(tidyverse)
library(wehoop)
library(cowplot)
library(ggrepel)
library(gt)

playerbox <- load_wbb_player_box(
  seasons = 2023)

big <- c("Nebraska", "Iowa", "Nortwestern", "Minnesota", "Wisconsin", "Illinois", "Purdue", "Indiana", "Michigan", "Michigan State", "Ohio State", "Rutgers", "Penn State", "Maryland")

bigplayerbox <- playerbox |>
  filter(team_short_display_name %in% big) |>
  group_by(athlete_display_name, team_short_display_name) |>
  summarize(
    minutes = sum(minutes, na.rm=TRUE),
    Points = sum(points, na.rm=TRUE),
    rebounds = sum(rebounds, na.rm=TRUE),
    turnovers = sum(turnovers, na.rm=TRUE),
    assists = sum(assists, na.rm=TRUE),
    blocks = sum(blocks, na.rm=TRUE),
    steals = sum(steals, na.rm=TRUE),
    fieldgoal = sum(field_goals_made, na.rm=TRUE),
    fieldgoalattempts = sum(field_goals_attempted, na.rm=TRUE),
    threesmade = sum(three_point_field_goals_made, na.rm=TRUE),
    threesattempted = sum(three_point_field_goals_attempted, na.rm=TRUE)) |>
  mutate(fgpct = fieldgoal/fieldgoalattempts, threepct = threesmade/threesattempted)

jaz <- bigplayerbox |>
  filter(athlete_display_name == "Jaz Shelley")

nebraska <- playerbox |>
  filter(team_short_display_name == "Nebraska") |>
  group_by(athlete_display_name, team_short_display_name) |>
  summarize(
    minutes = sum(minutes, na.rm=TRUE),
    Points = sum(points, na.rm=TRUE),
    rebounds = sum(rebounds, na.rm=TRUE),
    assists = sum(assists, na.rm=TRUE),
    steals = sum(steals, na.rm=TRUE),
    turnovers = sum(turnovers, na.rm=TRUE),
    blocks = sum(blocks, na.rm=TRUE),
    fieldgoal = sum(field_goals_made, na.rm=TRUE),
    fieldgoalattempts = sum(field_goals_attempted, na.rm=TRUE),
    threesmade = sum(three_point_field_goals_made, na.rm=TRUE),
    threesattempted = sum(three_point_field_goals_attempted, na.rm=TRUE)) |>
  mutate(fgpct = fieldgoal/fieldgoalattempts, threepct = threesmade/threesattempted) |>
  filter(minutes > 260)

The Australian is a dominating player in many aspects of the game. She led the team with a total of 206 assists, with the next leading player only at 59. She knows how to create good shots for her teammates.

Not only can she share the ball, but she is a lock down shooter making it hard to guard her. Shelley had knocked down 79 three-point shots, making it 237 of her 478 total points.

But what about her time at Oregon? Does she come close to how she is as a Husker?

Not only did Shelley’s playing time triple from moving schools, but she almost has made six times the number of threes as she did at Oregon. Shelley had only made 87 points during her last season at Oregon. And at Nebraska? 478 points last season.

Since she has five time as many of points at Nebraska than she did at Oregon, is she getting more opportunities at Nebraska? How does she rank amongst other Big Ten star players?

Jaz Shelley comes in third to most playing time in the Big Ten Conference following Iowa star, Caitlin Clark and Ohio State sharp-shooter, Taylor Mikesell. Shelley was only playing 388 minutes a season at Oregon and now is playing 1188 minutes. This means that she is able to take more shots and facilitate the ball more, hence the top rankings for each of those on the Husker team.

Shelley has excelled her game coming to Nebraska. She continues to dominate the game as she has entered her fifth and final season. The difference of her impact at Oregon from Nebraska is something incredible to look at. She has become a Cornhusker star.
